The constantly evolving state of recyclable plastics indicates that contamination plays an
increasingly important role in the amount of plastic that gets recycled. Contamination can negate
the recyclability of large volumes of material, often occurring accidentally. When plastics
containing residual food waste are mixed with clean recyclables, the entire batch can become
unsuitable for processing. Scaled across a global population of billions, such contamination
presents a challenge to achieving sustainable recycling systems. Artificial intelligence has become
a buzzword in the context of environmental conservation efforts and has the potential to alleviate
the harms and major costs associated with recycling in the modern day. Therefore, while using
previous research as a backbone, we explored whether a trained artificial intelligence model could
identify the level of contamination in a sample of plastic accurately with relatively low cost. In
the past, research of this sort has been applied to marine contexts but not as widely to
terrestrial situations, serving as a major research opportunity. We discovered that Convolutional
Neural Networks ( CNNs ) can be trained to analyze images of plastic samples and produce results
based on the present contamination in the material. By incorporating our AI model in modern
recycling facilities, the detection of contamination and the efficiency of plastic recycling as a
whole could dramatically improve at a fraction of the cost.
